About

The Policy

The proposed policy puts a fee of $30 per metric ton of carbon dioxide at the point of entry to New Jersey. For example, 10% ethanol gasoline outputs up to 17.6 pounds of carbon dioxide per gallon, so the fee on a gallon of gas is 17.6 pounds CO2 * (1 metric ton / 2205 pounds) * ($30/ 1 metric ton) = $0.24

The Cost

This fee is paid by energy producers and sellers. For example, companies like ExxonMobil or BP would pay the fee as gasoline is shipped into New Jersey.

While ordinary citizens won't directly pay the fee, companies almost surely will have to increase the price of affected products. In our calculator, we make the conservative assumption that the entire burden of the fee gets passed along to consumers.
